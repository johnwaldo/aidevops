## Creative Brief Templates

### Standard Creative Brief

```text
PROJECT: [Name]  |  DATE: [Date]  |  REQUESTER: [Name]  |  DUE: [Date]

1. OBJECTIVE: [Goal + KPI]
2. TARGET AUDIENCE: Demographics + psychographics + pain points
3. KEY MESSAGE: One thing they should know/feel/do
4. SUPPORTING MESSAGES: 2-3 points
5. TONE: Professional/Casual · Serious/Playful · Authoritative/Friendly
6. FORMAT: Image/Video(length)/Carousel(cards)/Story/Reel  |  PLATFORM: [Platform]
7. SPECS: Aspect ratio · Dimensions · File format · Max size · Length
8. MUST-HAVES: Logo · Product shot · Copy/tagline · Legal · Offer · CTA
9. BRAND: Colors · Fonts · Logo usage · Imagery style · Avoid
10. INSPIRATION: LOVE: [URLs]  |  AVOID: [URLs]
11. CTA: [e.g. "Start Free Trial"]
12. SUCCESS METRICS: Primary: [e.g. CPA <$50]  |  Secondary: [CTR, hook rate]
13. BUDGET: $___  |  DUE: [Date]  |  REVISIONS: [N]
14. APPROVAL: 1. [Name]  2. [Name]
```

### Video Brief

```text
PROJECT: [Name]  |  LENGTH: [15s/30s/60s]  |  PLATFORM: [Platform]
OBJECTIVE: [Goal]  |  AUDIENCE: [Who]

STRUCTURE:
  [0-3s]   HOOK: Scroll-stopper visual/verbal
  [3-10s]  PROBLEM: Pain point
  [10-25s] SOLUTION: Product resolves it
  [25-40s] PROOF: Testimonial / rating
  [40-45s] CTA: Desired action

STYLE: UGC/Professional/Hybrid/Animation/Screen recording
VISUAL: Setting · People · Props · B-roll
AUDIO: Music · Voiceover tone · Captions
DELIVERABLES: Final edited · Raw footage · Versions (16:9/9:16/1:1/4:5, with/without captions)
REVISIONS: [N]  |  DEADLINE: [Date]  |  BUDGET: $___
```

### UGC Creator Brief

```text
HI [CREATOR]! Thanks for working with us on [BRAND].

PRODUCT: [2-3 sentences — what it is, key differentiator]
VIEWER: [Who watches — demographics + struggle]
GOAL: [What video should accomplish]

STRUCTURE (40-45s):
  [0-3s]   HOOK: Relatable problem or surprising statement
  [3-10s]  YOUR STORY: Struggle before product
  [10-25s] DISCOVERY: How you found it, first impressions
  [25-40s] RESULTS: Specific ("First night, fell asleep in 20 min")
  [40-45s] RECOMMENDATION: Why others should try it

FILMING: Casual setting · Natural light · Vertical 9:16 · Eye level · Talk TO camera
DO: Personal details · Authentic delivery · Genuine recommendation
DON'T: Sound scripted · Use marketing language · Make medical claims

DELIVERABLES: All raw footage + 40-45s edited, vertical 9:16, clear audio
COMPENSATION: $[Amount]  |  USAGE: [Paid/Organic/Both]  |  DEADLINE: [Date]
```

### Performance Brief (Quick)

```text
CAMPAIGN: [Name]  |  OBJECTIVE: [Goal + KPI]  |  PLATFORM: [Where]  |  AUDIENCE: [Who]
HOOK: [First 3s direction]  |  BODY: [Key points]  |  CTA: [Action]
SPECS: Format · Size · Length · Due  |  INSPIRATION: [Links]  |  METRIC: [Target]
```

**Brief best practices:** Be specific (vague = mediocre) · Include visual examples · Define success metrics · Give context (why) · Allow creative freedom within guidelines · Define deliverables and approval process clearly. Never leave sections blank, skip specs, or give conflicting direction.

## Competitor Ad Analysis

**Research tools:**

| Tool | Cost | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| Facebook Ad Library | Free | All active FB/IG ads. 3+ months running = likely winner. |
| Google Ads Transparency | Free | Display/video only (not search). |
| SpyFu / SEMrush / Ahrefs | Paid | Search ads, keywords, bid data, historical copy, estimated spend. |
| AdEspresso / Foreplay | Paid | Curated libraries, performance indicators, creative trends. |

### Analysis Framework

```text
BASIC: Competitor · Date · Platform · Format · Still running?
CREATIVE: Hook · Value prop · Offer · Social proof · CTA · Visual style · Length
MESSAGING: Angle · Emotional trigger · Target audience · Differentiation
INSIGHTS: What to learn · How to do better · Gaps they leave
```

### Differentiation Strategy

After analysis, identify three layers:

1. **Commoditized** (everyone says it) — say it better or differently
2. **Gaps** (nobody says it) — own that messaging
3. **Unique** (only you can claim) — lead with this

| Competitor Says | You Say (Better) |
|-----------------|------------------|
| "Easy to use" | "So easy your team will actually use it — 4.9-star rating" |
| "Manage projects" | "See everything in one view — no more chaos" |
| "Try free" | "Try free 60 days, no credit card, cancel anytime" |

### Monthly Audit Process (1-2h, first Monday)

1. **Research (30m):** FB Ad Library + Google Transparency for all competitors, screenshot new ads
2. **Document (30m):** Add to swipe file (competitor + date), note format/messaging/offers/hooks
3. **Analyze (30m):** Cross-competitor trends, messaging gaps, new offers
4. **Act (30m):** Create 3-5 test concepts, plan adaptations (not copies), update positioning

**Tracking columns:** Competitor · Date · Platform · Format · Hook · Message · Offer · CTA · Screenshot · Status · Months running · Our response

**Ethics:** Research public ads, get inspired (not copy), identify gaps. Never copy verbatim, steal assets, or use exact messaging.

## Seasonal and Trending Creative

### Seasonal Calendar

| Quarter | Key Events |
|---------|------------|
| Q1 | New Year's · Valentine's · Spring Break · Super Bowl |
| Q2 | Easter · Mother's Day · Father's Day · Graduation · Pride |
| Q3 | Independence Day · Prime Day · Back to School · Labor Day |
| Q4 | Halloween · Black Friday/Cyber Monday · Christmas · New Year's Eve |

**Planning timeline:** 6-8w: concept approval → 4-6w: production → 2-4w: launch → monitor → analyze

**Per-event template:** `[EVENT]: Date · Audience · Angle · Offer · Assets · Launch date · Budget · Metrics`

**Best practices:**

- Don't force it — seasonal angle must make sense for product
- Budget mix: Evergreen 60-70% + Seasonal 30-40%
- Refresh options: full rebrand / seasonal offer only / minimal copy mention

**Seasonal examples:**

- **BFCM:** Bold text, discount badges, countdown timers. Messaging: clear discounts, urgency, scarcity.
- **New Year:** Fresh imagery, before/after, motivational. Angle: resolution, fresh start.
- **Valentine's:** Gift angle or self-love angle. Include delivery deadline.
- **Back to School:** Organization-focused, parent relief or student discount.

### Trending Creative

**Trend lifecycles:** Viral meme: 3-7d · Platform trend: 2-4w · Cultural event: 1-2w · News: 1-3d (or avoid)

**Process:** Spot early → 24-48h turnaround → launch while relevant → kill when trend dies

**Brand-trend fit check:** Aligns with brand? Audience finds it relevant? Adds value (not just piggybacks)? Safe? If NO to any: skip.

**Newsjacking:** Only positive/industry-relevant/non-controversial news. Never tragedies or political topics.

## Retargeting Creative Strategy

### Funnel Segments

| Segment | Who | Window | Strategy | Example Message |
|---------|-----|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Site Visitors | Visited, didn't convert | 1-30d | Remind, address objections, social proof | "Still thinking about it? Here's what you should know..." |
| Engaged Visitors | 2+ pages, 2+ min, video watched | 1-14d | Differentiation, stronger offers, case studies | "You spent 5 min on [Product]. Here's why 10K+ chose us." |
| Cart Abandoners | Added to cart, didn't buy | 1-7d | Final objections, time-limited discount, urgency | "You left [Product] in your cart! Save 10% in next 24h." |
| Past Customers | Already purchased | Varies | Reorder, upsell/cross-sell, loyalty, referral | "Time to reorder!" / "Customers who bought A love B — 30% off." |

### Best Practices

1. **Acknowledge the relationship** — "Welcome back!" not "Discover our product!"
2. **Dynamic creative** — Show products they viewed (FB Dynamic Product Ads, Google Dynamic Remarketing)
3. **Progression messaging** — Day 1-3: features → Day 4-7: testimonials → Day 8-14: 15% off → Day 15-30: 20% off
4. **Objection handling:** Site visitors: ROI/value + testimonials. Cart abandoners: discount/payment plans + free shipping.
5. **Offer escalation** — Week 1: no offer → Week 2: 10% → Week 3: 20% → Week 4+: 30% + bonus. Don't train them to wait.
6. **Frequency caps** — Site visitors: 3-4/day. Engaged: 4-5/day. Cart: 5-6/day first 48h then reduce. Max window: 30-90 days. Exclude converters immediately.

### Creative Formulas

| Formula | Structure |
|---------|-----------|
| Reminder + Social Proof | Reminder → proof → CTA |
| Objection Handling | Acknowledge hesitation → address → proof → reduce risk |
| Urgency/Scarcity | Acknowledge delay → urgency → consequence → CTA |
| Value Stack | Product reminder → everything included → total value vs. price → CTA |
| Comparison | Acknowledge research → differentiation → social proof |

### Platform Notes

**Facebook/Instagram:** Segment by website visitors (180d), engagement (365d), customer list, app activity. Video viewers 50%+: skip re-intro, focus on offer. Email subscribers: exclusive offers.

**Google:** RLSA (bid higher for past visitors), Display remarketing (dynamic, rotate, frequency cap), YouTube (explainer viewers → testimonial → offer).

**Email sequence:** 1h: "Forget something?" + cart → 24h: 10% off + objections → 48h: "Last chance" + stronger discount → 7d: "We miss you! 20% off"

### Performance Benchmarks

| Segment | CTR vs Cold | CPA vs Cold | Conv Rate |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| Cart Abandoners | 2-4x higher | 30-50% lower | 10-30% |
| Engaged Visitors | 2-4x higher | 30-50% lower | 5-15% |
| Site Visitors | 2-4x higher | 30-50% lower | 2-8% |

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Retargeting too broadly | Segment by intent (product viewers > blog readers) |
| Same ad forever | Creative rotation + progressive messaging |
| No frequency cap | Cap 3-5x/day, rotate creative |
| Same offer as cold traffic | Stronger offers for warm audience |
| Not excluding converters | Exclude conversion event audience |
| Retargeting too long | 30-60 days for most products (intent decays) |

## Ad Fatigue Prevention

**Symptoms:** CTR declining >20% WoW · CPA increasing >25% · Frequency >5 · Relevance score dropping

**Fatigue timeline:** Cold: 7-14d · Warm: 14-30d · Small audiences (<50K): faster · Large (>1M): slower

### Prevention Strategies

**1. Creative Rotation** — Always run 5+ variations: 1 control + 2 iterations + 2 new concepts. Weekly: add 2-3 new, remove bottom 2, keep 5-7 active.

**2. Iterate vs. New Concept**

- *Iterate* (change ONE element) when performing well but frequency rising: different creator, hook, text, length, visual style
- *New concept* (entirely new angle) when fatiguing badly or reaching new segment: endorsement, demo, results montage, comparison, educational

**3. Audience Expansion** — Lookalike 1% → 2-3% → 5-10% · Add related interests · Geo expansion

**4. Frequency Management**

| Audience | Cap |
|----------|-----|
| Cold | 4-5/week |
| Warm | 6-8/week |
| Hot | 8-10/week |

Triggers: Frequency >4 + declining CTR → add creatives. Frequency >6 + rising CPA → expand audience. Frequency >8 → pause and refresh.

**5. Creative Lifespan** — Day 1-14: launch/learning → Day 15-30: peak → Day 31-45: optimal ROI → Day 46+: decline. At day 30: launch replacement. At day 45: shift budget. At day 60: retire.

**6. Hook Swapping** — Keep video body (3-60s), swap first 3s. Film 5-10 hooks, test as separate ads. Extends lifespan at low cost.

**7. Platform Diversification** — FB/IG 60% · Google 20% · TikTok/LinkedIn/YouTube 20%

### Fatigue Response

| Severity | Symptoms | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Light | CTR down 10-20% or frequency 4-5 | Add 2 creatives, expand audience 20% |
| Moderate | CTR down 20-30%, CPA up 15-25%, frequency 5-7 | Add 3-5 creatives + expansion, pause fatigued ad 48-72h |
| Severe | CTR down 30%+, CPA up 25%+, frequency 7+ | Kill immediately, launch 5+ replacements |

### Fatigue-Resistant Tactics

1. **Evergreen angles** — Timeless value props, not time-sensitive creative
2. **Variety in sameness** — 10 variations of winning message: different creators, styles, hooks
3. **Modular video** — 10 hooks × 3 body segments × 5 CTAs = 150 combinations
4. **Dynamic creative** — Facebook DCO, Google Responsive Ads for automatic rotation
5. **Sequential messaging** — Impression 1: problem → 2: solution → 3: proof → 4: offer

### Production Volume

| Spend/Month | Monthly Creative Need |
|-------------|----------------------|
| $10K | 20-30 creatives |
| $50K | 50-100 creatives |
| $200K+ | 100-200+ creatives |

**Production mix:** UGC ($100-300/video, 10-20 creators = 20-60/month) · In-house (30-50/month) · Agency retainer (20-40/month, $5K-20K) · Hybrid (in-house iterations + UGC authenticity + agency hero)

## Thumbnail Design

**The 1-second rule:** Viewer decides in 1 second. Clickable thumbnails need: visual intrigue · emotional trigger · curiosity gap · contrast · clarity at small size.

### Design Elements

**Faces** — Close-up (40-60% of frame), exaggerated expressions, direct eye contact, high contrast. Avoid tiny faces, neutral expressions, looking away.

| Emotion | Use For |
|---------|---------|
| Shock/Surprise | Revealing results, unexpected outcomes |
| Excitement | Success stories, wins |
| Curiosity | Questions, mysteries, teasers |
| Frustration | Problem-focused content |
| Smiling | Tutorials, positive content |

**Text overlays** — 3-6 words max, bold thick fonts (Impact, Montserrat Bold, Bebas Neue), high contrast (white + black outline), top/bottom third, never cover face.

Text formulas: `"$10K in 30 Days"` · `"How I [Result]"` · `"[A] vs. [B]"` · `"Don't [Mistake]"` · `"Why [Surprising Fact]?"`

**Visual elements** — Arrows (bright red/yellow) · Circles/highlighting · Before/after split · Product in use

**Color theory:**

| Color | Emotion | Contrasts With |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Red | Urgency | White, black, yellow |
| Yellow | Attention | Black, purple, blue |
| Blue | Trust | White, orange, yellow |
| Green | Growth | White, black |
| Purple | Luxury | Yellow, white |
| Orange | Energy | Blue, black |

High contrast pairs: Black+White · Yellow+Black · Red+White · Blue+Orange. Avoid: Yellow+White · Light blue+Light green.

### Platform Specs

**YouTube:** 1280×720 min, 16:9, <2MB, JPG/PNG. Max 3 elements, face 40-60%, small logo corner.

**Facebook/Instagram:** Square 1080×1080 · Vertical 1080×1920 · Landscape 1920×1080. Assume sound-off, larger text for mobile, native feel. Keep elements out of UI zones (top/bottom 250px for Stories/Reels).

**TikTok/Shorts:** 9:16 only. First frame = thumbnail. Start with hook visual, text overlay, bright high contrast.

### Thumbnail Templates

| Template | Layout | Best For |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Face + Text | Face 50% one side, 3-6 word text opposite | Personal brand, testimonials |
| Split Screen | Left: Before/A, Right: After/B, "VS" center | Comparisons, transformations |
| Text-Dominant | Bold color background, text 60-70%, small image | Listicles, quick tips |
| Over-the-Shoulder | Person looking at screen, arrow to key element | Tutorials, reviews |
| Reaction | Large expressive face + result text | Results, surprising facts |

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Tiny text | Increase size, reduce word count. Test at display size. |
| Too cluttered | Max 3 elements |
| Low contrast | Add stroke/outline, change background or text color |
| Misleading | Accurately represent content |
| Generic stock | Custom or authentic images |
| No human element | Add face with emotion (except product demos) |
| Inconsistent branding | Establish template (fonts, colors, layout) |

### Advanced Tactics

1. **Series branding** — Consistent template across series (same layout/colors, only text changes)
2. **Curiosity gaps** — Blurred areas, arrows to blank space, partial information
3. **Social proof** — "1M+ Views" · "5-star Rated" · "As Seen On [Publication]"
4. **Negative space** — Simple clean designs often outperform busy ones
5. **Movement suggestion** — Motion blur, mid-action pose, directional arrows
